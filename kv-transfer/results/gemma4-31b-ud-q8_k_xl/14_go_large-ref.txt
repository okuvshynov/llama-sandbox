### 1. Critical Bugs & Race Conditions

#### A. Concurrent Map Access (The "Handlers" Race)
**Issue:** In `workerpool.go`, the `Worker.Run()` method accesses `w.pool.scheduler.handlers[task.Name]` without any synchronization. Simultaneously, `Scheduler.RegisterHandler` modifies this map under a lock.
**Impact:** This will cause a panic (`fatal error: concurrent map read and map write`) in production when a handler is registered while tasks are being executed.
**Fix:** Use a `sync.RWMutex` in the `Scheduler` to protect the `handlers` map and call `s.mu.RLock()` before accessing the handler in the worker loop.

#### B. The "Retry" Goroutine Leak
**Issue:** In `CompleteTask`, when a task fails and is eligible for retry, a goroutine is spawned: `go func() { ... time.Sleep(...) ... s.mu.Lock() ... }`. 
**Impact:** 
1. **Zombie Goroutines:** If the `Scheduler` is stopped via `Stop()`, these goroutines continue to live until their sleep timer expires.
2. **Race Condition:** The goroutine accesses `task` and `s.mu` after a long delay. If the task was deleted by `cleanupCompletedTasks` during the sleep, the goroutine will attempt to push a "ghost" task back into the heap.
**Fix:** Use a `time.Timer` and a centralized "delay queue" or check if the scheduler is still running/task still exists before re-queueing.

#### C. Recursive Stack Overflow in `AssignTask`
**Issue:** In `AssignTask`, if the popped task is `TaskCancelled`, the function calls itself recursively: `return s.AssignTask(workerID)`.
**Impact:** If a large batch of tasks is cancelled (e.g., 10,000 tasks), the scheduler will hit a stack overflow and crash.
**Fix:** Replace the recursion with a `for` loop.
