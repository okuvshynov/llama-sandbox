### 1. Critical Bugs & Logic Errors

#### A. The "Delete All" Reservation Bug
In `inventory.py`, the `cleanup_expired_reservations` method is catastrophically broken.
*   **Issue:** It iterates through all reservations and adds them to the `expired` list regardless of their timestamp, then deletes them all.
*   **Impact:** Every time this cleanup runs, **all pending orders lose their stock reservations**, leading to overselling and order failures.
*   **Fix:** Use the `reservation_timeout` to check the actual timestamp of the reservation before adding it to the deletion list.

#### B. The "Lost Stock" on Cancellation
In `order_processor.py`, the `cancel_order` method handles refunds but forgets inventory.
*   **Issue:** If an order has already been processed (`OrderStatus.CONFIRMED` or `SHIPPED`), the stock has already been deducted from `product.stock_count` via `commit_reservation`. When `cancel_order` is called, it refunds the money but **never calls `inventory.restock()`**.
*   **Impact:** Inventory counts will drift downward permanently, showing items as "out of stock" when they are actually sitting in the warehouse.
*   **Fix:** In `cancel_order`, if the order was already committed, iterate through the items and call `self.inventory.restock(item.product.id, item.quantity)`.

#### C. The "Payment without Stock" Race Condition
In `order_processor.py`, `process_next` charges the customer *before* committing the reservation.
*   **Issue:** If `self.inventory.commit_reservation(order.id)` fails (e.g., due to a crash or a logic error), the customer has been charged, but the order is not confirmed. The `Exception` block releases the reservation but **does not refund the payment**.
*   **Impact:** Customers are charged for orders that are never fulfilled.
*   **Fix:** Use a **Try-Except-Else** block or a Distributed Transaction pattern. If the commit fails, an immediate refund must be triggered.
